Developing User Profiles and Journey

After initially meeting with the client, I generally take notes about what the client is asking for, with as much detail as possible. Using that, I develop a user journey in order to see how many views are needed and how each user would interact with each other, with the potential functionalities that each would be able to access.

Creating on High Level User Journeys and Designs

Here is where I lay out the happy paths and the other outcomes during a particular flow, along with a description of the functionality of the things within that screen. Developing and Iterating on Detailed Wireframes

Once the flows have been viewed and generally approved by the client, I then will move on and create a more detailed version of the total journey. In order to do this, I leverage tools such as Axure, Powerpoint, or Balsamiq. These interactive wireframes will mimic the potential functionality, look, and feel of a real application with the appropriate branding. Then, the client will get a more comprehensive understanding of how we intend to proceed so that we are able to display the wireframes to our internal teams and begin to write requirements for the product.

Writing User Stories

Once the prototype has reached a stable state, I can begin creating a structure out of the project requirements. Based on the functionality that we had previously determined, I can break down the entire product into different components, down to user stories. Once I write the requirements and stories for the front-end pieces of the product, I then will begin writing the back end pieces of it. This is an iterative process with constant coordination with the UX and development teams to ensure that all pieces of the application are accounted for and met, and the process to finish detailing the requirements will continue as discussions persist during the execution process.
